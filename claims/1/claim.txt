================================================================
Claim 1 — Cross-source detection generalization (paper §1)
================================================================

Statement
----------------------------------------------------------------
Our per-language fine-tuned OpenAI Privacy Filter (OPF)
checkpoints beat Microsoft Presidio (industry NER baseline) on
held-out cross-source PII corpora across all three evaluated
languages, under projection-symmetric scoring.

Headline numbers from the paper (n=1000 per corpus)
----------------------------------------------------------------
  Micro F1 delta (regex+OPF vs. regex+Presidio):
    English  (nemotron_en):    +0.110
    German   (ai4privacy_de):  +0.059
    Russian  (wolframko_ru):   +0.051

  The OPF win holds in every language tested.

Acceptance bounds
----------------------------------------------------------------
SMOKE mode (default, --smoke, ~10 min, n=200 per corpus):
  - All 9 evaluation cells (3 corpora × 3 detector configs)
    complete without error.
  - For each language, micro F1 of `regex_opf__<lang>` exceeds
    micro F1 of `regex_presidio` by at least 0.025 (i.e. half
    the smallest paper delta, allowing for 200-row subsample
    variance).
  - The relative ranking (OPF > Presidio > regex_only on every
    language) is preserved.

  Note on Presidio reproducibility: the `regex_presidio` cells
  may not bit-exactly match expected/smoke/ — Presidio routes
  PERSON / LOCATION / DATE recognizers through spaCy NER, whose
  per-token scores vary across `en_core_web_lg` / `de_core_news_lg`
  / `ru_core_news_lg` releases. Per-placeholder TP/FP counts can
  shift by single-digit amounts and micro F1 by ±0.02 between
  spaCy versions. The acceptance bound above (`+0.025` OPF lead)
  is set wide enough to absorb that variance. `regex_only` and
  `regex_opf` cells are deterministic and should match exactly
  modulo elapsed-time numbers.

FULL mode (--full, ~3-4 h, ~$0.05 OpenRouter):
  - All 21 cells (3 corpora × 7 detector configs) complete
    without error at n=1000.
  - Each cell's macro/micro F1 falls within the 95% CI of the
    matching cell in claims/1/expected/full/.
  - regex_opf beats regex_presidio on every language by at
    least the paper deltas above (within CI).

What `run.sh` does
----------------------------------------------------------------
Smoke: runs run_imported_dataset_eval.py 9 times — each of the
three corpora (nemotron_en, ai4privacy_de, wolframko_ru) against
each of three configs (regex_only, regex_opf with the language-
matched fine-tune, regex_presidio). 200 rows per cell, capped at
600 raw rows to keep the run under 10 minutes.

Full: runs the full 21-cell matrix at 1000 rows per cell via
benchmarks/pii_evaluation/run_section1_matrix_n1000.sh. Adds four
small LLM detector backends per corpus:
  nvidia/nemotron-nano-9b-v2  qwen/qwen3.5-9b
  google/gemma-3-4b-it        mistralai/ministral-3b-2512

Smoke has no API cost (all detectors run locally). Full requires
PII_EVAL_API_KEY set to an OpenRouter key for the four LLM cells
(~$0.05 total across the 12 LLM cells in the matrix).
